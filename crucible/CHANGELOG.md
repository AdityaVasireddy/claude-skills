# Changelog

All notable changes to Crucible are documented in this file.

The format follows [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.1.0/),
and versioning follows [Semantic Versioning](https://semver.org/).

## [Unreleased]

### Changed

- **Stage 4 (Evidence) methodology** refined into four explicit
  passes — Discovery (existence), Adequacy, Remaining Wedge
  Opportunities, and Market State — so "competitors exist" can never
  be read as "the problem is solved." Existence answers only "does
  this capability already exist?"; adequacy independently answers
  "is the underlying problem actually well solved for this buyer?",
  including the mandatory falsification question ("what evidence
  would convince us this market is not solved despite existing
  competitors?") and the required statement "No evidence collected
  regarding adequacy" when the pass comes up empty (the adequacy
  assumption then enters the stack as `HOPE`). Market state must be
  classified as one of Sparse / Emerging / Crowded-but-Weak /
  Crowded-with-Strong-Incumbents / Mature / Commodity with cited
  evidence, or declared "insufficient evidence to classify." Evidence
  tiers, stack tags, ledger schema, and all other stages are
  unchanged; the worked example's Stage 4 was updated to show the new
  passes.

## [2.0.0] — 2026-07-17

Crucible v2 is a ground-up redesign. It replaces the v1 judging
council with an assumption-driven evidence loop that never predicts
outcomes and compounds founder judgment across sessions via a
persistent ledger. This is a breaking change with no backward
compatibility — v1 and v2 sessions produce incompatible output shapes
and are documented separately (see `docs/MIGRATION_V1_TO_V2.md`).

### Added

- **`docs/PHILOSOPHY.md`** — the constitution: what Crucible is and
  is not, the problem it solves, who it's for, and seven invariant
  principles ("Judge arguments, not outcomes," "Reality owns
  empirical truth," "The founder owns the bet," among others) meant
  to survive all future versions.
- **`docs/ARCHITECTURE.md`** — the three-judges ownership model (LLM
  / reality / founder), the six system components and their single
  responsibilities, the data-flow diagram, session and ledger
  lifecycles, and explicit degradation modes for missing search or
  filesystem access.
- **`docs/WORKFLOW.md`** — the six-stage session workflow
  (Input → Interrogation → Bench → Evidence → Experiment → Ledger)
  documented stage by stage: what enters, what happens, what leaves.
- **`docs/EVALUATION.md`** — a founder-judgment evaluation framework:
  eight measurable indicators (stack health, founder calibration,
  learning velocity, pre-commitment follow-through, interrogation
  delta, and three system-discipline indicators), explicitly scoped
  away from venture-outcome measurement.
- **`docs/MIGRATION_V1_TO_V2.md`** — the full account of what changed
  from v1 and why, including a table of every persona/mechanism
  mapped to its v2 replacement.
- **The Ledger** — a persistent per-venture record
  (`.crucible/<venture-slug>.md`) that carries the assumption stack,
  experiment records (with predictions and pre-commitments), and a
  session log, so founder calibration compounds across sessions
  instead of resetting on every run.
- **Session log** — a ledger field recording each session's Bench
  verdict and evidence-tier counts, making the system-discipline
  evaluation indicators computable from the ledger alone.
- **Prediction + pre-commitment protocol** — before every experiment,
  the founder states an expected outcome and what they'll do if the
  experiment fails its threshold; the following session opens by
  scoring the prediction and checking the pre-commitment.
- **Warranted Bet answer** — a designed response for when a founder
  asks an outcome question ("should I build it?"): what's known vs.
  hoped, the spend current evidence justifies, and the founder's own
  calibration record — instead of an improvised verdict.

### Changed

- **Judgment model** — the LLM now judges only what is verifiable
  immediately from stated facts (arithmetic, contradictions,
  incentive alignment, structural impossibility). Everything
  empirical is deferred to reality via experiments.
- **Workflow** — a single-pass five-persona-then-Judge pipeline became
  a six-stage loop with an external edge: the experiment outcome is
  generated by the world, between sessions, and feeds the next
  session's Input stage.
- **Experiment model** — one 48-hour test became one experiment per
  cycle, typed to the risk being tested (demand / distribution /
  technical / regulatory) and cost-capped to the founder's stated
  affordable loss rather than a flat time box.
- **Council composition** — five judging personas (Contrarian,
  Expansionist, Logician, Researcher, Buyer) became three
  question-asking examiners (Skeptic, Operator, Buyer) whose output is
  the assumption stack, not a verdict.
- **Evidence gathering** — live search changed from optional (used by
  one persona) to mandatory when available, with every factual claim
  now tiered `VERIFIED` / `RECALL` / `REASONED`.
- **Intake** — added the founder's goal (lifestyle vs. venture-scale)
  and affordable loss, which the original council never asked for and
  silently assumed.
- **Scope** — Crucible now requires a customer and a revenue path;
  pure research/frontier bets get an explicit out-of-scope response
  instead of an improvised verdict.

### Removed

- Five-persona judging council (Contrarian, Expansionist, Logician,
  Researcher) as verdict-issuing seats.
- The Judge role and cross-persona verdict synthesis.
- `GO` / `RESHAPE` / `KILL` verdicts on the venture, and veto floors.
- All numeric scoring (1–10 persona scores, confidence levels).
- The flat 48-hour test-time requirement (generalized into a
  cost-capped, risk-typed experiment).
- Persona role-play treated as evidence anywhere in the system.
- Any claim of predicting venture outcomes.

### Fixed

*(Findings from the pre-release architecture review — see
`docs/MIGRATION_V1_TO_V2.md` for full rationale.)*

- `docs/EVALUATION.md` claimed every indicator was computable from
  the ledger alone; two indicators required transcript data the
  ledger didn't carry. Added the session log to the ledger schema so
  the claim holds for all but one (now explicitly marked manual)
  indicator.
- `docs/EVALUATION.md`'s interrogation-delta indicator described a
  founder self-assessment step the implementation never performs;
  reworded as an explicit manual, optional practice rather than an
  automated behavior.
- Ledger experiment-heading format disagreed between `SKILL.md`'s
  template and the worked example; unified to `E<n> — S<session>`.
- `docs/WORKFLOW.md` described the experiment quality bar as
  "unchanged" from v1 when one criterion (the time cap) had in fact
  been generalized; corrected the description.
- The returning-session re-entry point (loop resumes at Bench,
  skipping Interrogation) was specified only in `SKILL.md`; added to
  `docs/ARCHITECTURE.md`'s session lifecycle as the document of
  record, with `docs/WORKFLOW.md` cross-referencing it.
- Terminology drift: "Warranted Bet statement" vs. "answer," and
  "Ledger" vs. "Ledger update" as a stage name — unified across all
  documents.
- Worked example's in-session assumption-stack table used a
  differently named column than the ledger's canonical table; aligned.
- Added a precedence note to `docs/WORKFLOW.md` stating that stage
  semantics are owned by `docs/ARCHITECTURE.md`, to prevent the two
  documents drifting into competing definitions over time.
